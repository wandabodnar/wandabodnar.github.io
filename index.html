Wanda Bodnar

Marine Geospatial Scientist & SUP Instructor

I am an experienced marine geospatial scientist working within the Greater Thames Estuary (UK) environment sector.
Also, I lead environment-focused stand up paddleboarding sessions on the Thames.

Project Officer

Thames Estuary Partnership

I developed and oversaw the Fish Migration Roadmap (link) project, which focused on a whole-system approach to help reprioritise fish migratory barriers and provide a blueprint for sustainable river restoration works within the Greater Thames Estuary and the Thames River Basin.

January 2018 - March 2021

Guest Lecturer

Regent's University London

Delivered lectures on environmental pollution and marine mammal mitigation methods used during the construction of offshore wind farms as part of the Environmental Sustainability course.

May 2015 - December 2017

Marine Environmental Consultant

Freelance

As a consultant I worked on implementing marine mammal monitoring and mitigation plans according to government and industry regulations for the offshore energy and renewables sector.
Tasks included: deployment of acoustic deterrence devices (ADDs) and real-time underwater noise monitoring during the construction of offshore windfarms and substations; visual (MMO) and passive acoustic (PAM) monitoring of marine mammal activity during sesimic surveys.

April 2015 - December 2017

Research Assistant

Royal Netherlands Institute for Sea Research

Following the successful completion of my internship, I was working as a research assistant to study the decline of balthic tellin (Macoma balthica) population at spatial and temporal scale in the western Dutch Wadden Sea.

April 2014 - March 2015

Research Intern

Royal Netherlands Institute for Sea Research

My internship included processing and analysing benthic macrofauna samples, field sampling and report writing. In addition, I participated in a pelagic sampling campaign in the Oosterschelde, and migratory shorebird catching and ringing in the Wadden Sea.

April 2014 - March 2015
